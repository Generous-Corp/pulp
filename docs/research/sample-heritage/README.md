# Sample Heritage mechanism research

These notes bound what Pulp's neutral Sample Heritage vocabulary can honestly
claim. They are mechanism notes, not product profiles or claims of emulation.
They use three evidence labels:

- **Confirmed** — stated by a cited primary source or directly defined by the
  linked Pulp schema and implementation.
- **Probable** — a defensible interpretation, but insufficient evidence for a
  device-specific claim.
- **Test** — an authoring hypothesis or calibration choice that needs a render,
  measurement, capture, or listening comparison before promotion.

The five narrow reviews are:

1. [Converters and coding laws](converters-and-coding-laws.md)
2. [Reconstruction and record filters](filters.md)
3. [Sampler pitch engines](pitch-engines.md)
4. [Noise and idle behavior](noise-and-idle.md)
5. [Cyclic and adaptive stretch](cyclic-stretch.md)

Hardware captures are useful calibration evidence but are optional for using
the neutral mechanisms. A named profile needs its own lawful evidence and
provenance; none is supplied here. Product and company names appear only to
identify sources. All trademarks belong to their respective owners. Pulp is not
affiliated with or endorsed by those owners.
