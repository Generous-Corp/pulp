# Sample Heritage mechanism research

These notes bound what Pulp's neutral Sample Heritage vocabulary can honestly
claim. They are mechanism notes, not product profiles or claims of emulation.
They use three evidence labels:

- **Confirmed** — stated by a cited primary source or directly defined by the
  linked Pulp schema and implementation.
- **Probable** — a defensible interpretation, but insufficient evidence for a
  device-specific claim.
- **Test** — an authoring hypothesis or calibration choice that needs a render,
  measurement, capture, or listening comparison before promotion.

The five narrow reviews are:

1. [Converters and coding laws](converters-and-coding-laws.md)
2. [Reconstruction and record filters](filters.md)
3. [Sampler pitch engines](pitch-engines.md)
4. [Noise and idle behavior](noise-and-idle.md)
5. [Cyclic and adaptive stretch](cyclic-stretch.md)

Hardware captures are useful calibration evidence but are optional for using
the neutral mechanisms. A named profile needs its own lawful evidence and
provenance; none is supplied here. Product and company names appear only to
identify sources. All trademarks belong to their respective owners. Pulp is not
affiliated with or endorsed by those owners.

## Reference-machine source set

The mechanism notes also audit five historically useful reference machines.
They are research targets, not profile names and not claims of exact emulation.
The primary-source set is:

- E-mu Systems SP-1200: [owner's manual](https://www.autistici.org/2000-maniax/texts/sp1200_manual.pdf)
  and [1987 service manual](https://archive.org/details/emu-sp-1200-service-manual-1987_202010).
- Akai S950: [operator's manual](https://manuals.fdiskc.com/flat/Akai%20S-950%20Owners%20Manual.pdf)
  and [service manual](https://www.synthxl.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/AKAI-950-service-manual.pdf).
- Akai S1100: [operator's manual transcript](https://manualzz.com/doc/14446171/akai-s1100-sampler-user-manual)
  and [service manual](https://manualzz.com/doc/6408169/akai-s1100-digital-sampler-service-manual).
- Akai S612: [service manual, part 1](https://www.florian-anwander.de/akai_s612/Akai_S-612_Service_Manual_Part_1.pdf)
  and [schematics/service bulletins, part 2](https://www.florian-anwander.de/akai_s612/Akai_S-612_Service_Manual_Part_2.pdf).
- Roland S-550: [official owner's manual](https://cdn.roland.com/assets/media/pdf/S-550_OM.pdf),
  [official S-330/S-550 basics guide](https://cdn.roland.com/assets/media/pdf/S330_550Basics.pdf),
  and [January 1988 service notes](https://archive.org/details/roland_S-550_SERVICE_NOTES).

Manual specifications establish architecture and author-facing ranges. They do
not replace captures for transfer curves, clock-dependent spectra, filter
coefficients, output-stage noise, or unit-to-unit tolerance. Each machine note
therefore identifies the smallest remaining **Test** set rather than filling
those gaps from reputation.
